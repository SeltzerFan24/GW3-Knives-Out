SET DESIGN

To ensure that Knives Out is an immersive experience, Writer/Director Rian Johnson and Producer Ram Bergman wisely relied on Production Designer David Crank, Set Decorator David Schlesinger SDSA and their teams, setting the bar at high-level creativity...and fun!

  • Harlan’s office, desktop... We’ve come full circle in this set and find equally fascinating objects.
  • Harlan’s private study... “A lot happens in this small room, and blocking played a big part of how we dressed it.”
  • Thrombey library, the Wheel of Knives.”
  • "Eventually we came up with building an armature to hold the knives and hanging it off of chain from the balcony."
  • The Thrombey estate consisted of two separate locations and a built set. We spent weeks shooting in each of the locations. Each of these spaces had unique challenges.
  • Harlan’s office... Harlan [Christopher Plummer] shares a gleeful moment with his nurse/caregiver, Marta [Ana de Armas]
  • "I gathered up a selection of Key Objects, and Rian created book titles to coordinate with each one.”
  • Harlan’s office, Visitor’s area
  • Harlan’s office, one corner of this amazing space
  • Harlan’s office, a closer look
  • “I was hesitant to use magic posters, as they are so often reproduced, but when I stumbled upon a collection of original posters, I could not refuse.”
  • "It was important to make these spaces feel unique and existing for separate purposes. The upstairs study is where he writes. It is Harlan’s private space.”
